Physical vs Digital Security

Your Business Has Two Sites to Protect: Physical and Digital

Directors rarely leave a storefront unlocked overnight. The same discipline must apply to endpoints, accounts, and cloud workspaces.

Locked doors in real life = protected endpoints online

A phishing message can steal credentials and open digital doors just like a stolen key opens a physical office.

Locked cabinets in real life = protected SaaS data

Ransomware can encrypt files in shared drives and business platforms, freezing invoicing, operations, and delivery.

Night guard in real life = monitored detection online

Continuous monitoring catches suspicious movement early so incidents are contained before they become business crises.

Director FAQ

Frequent Objections, Straight Answers

Clear answers for non-technical decision-makers before budget and vendor decisions.

We are a small company. Are we really a target?

Yes. Attackers often automate campaigns against SMEs because entry is easier and response resources are limited.

Will an MSSP replace our internal IT person?

No. DPaX works in a co-managed model. Your internal IT keeps ownership of business systems while we add specialist detection and response capability.

Can we start with a controlled budget and contract scope?

Yes. We define a right-sized first-phase contract for high-risk assets, then expand only when value is demonstrated.

What do SIEM, EDR, phishing, and ransomware mean in plain terms?

SIEM is a central alert console, EDR watches each device, phishing steals credentials through fake messages, and ransomware encrypts data to stop activity.
