Purpose of the call

Qualify your business exposure quickly and decide the right first cybersecurity step, without wasting time.

What this call is not

This is not a long technical audit. It is a decision call that frames risk, priorities, and contract scope.

Who should attend

Director or owner, and if possible your internal IT contact for implementation context.

Minute-by-Minute Script

Detailed 30-Minute Agenda

You always know where we are in the conversation and why each part matters for business decisions.

0-5 min: Business context and critical operations

We identify which activities cannot stop: invoicing, production, client access, ERP, and key shared files.

5-10 min: Current exposure map

We map your digital doors (accounts, remote access, workstations) and digital filing cabinets (SaaS and shared data).

10-15 min: Incident scenarios in plain language

We test realistic scenarios like phishing account takeover or ransomware encryption and estimate operational impact.

15-20 min: Existing controls and gaps

We check what is already in place (antivirus, backups, IT support) and where response speed or visibility is missing.

20-25 min: First-step options

We compare practical options: SOC monitoring, pentest mission, or a phased combination based on your priorities.

25-30 min: Decision summary and next action

We recap priorities, expected outcomes, timeline, and what happens immediately after the call.

How to Prepare

What to Bring to the Call

No technical dossier is required. This short checklist helps us produce a better first recommendation.

  • Your top 2-3 business processes that cannot stop.
  • Any recent incidents, suspicious emails, or service outages.
  • Main tools in use: Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, ERP, CRM, remote access, etc.
  • Current support model: internal IT only, external MSP, or mixed setup.
  • Budget constraints and desired timeline for first action.

If you do not have all details, that is fine. We start with what you know and refine after the call.

Plain-Language Glossary

Technical Terms We Explain During the Call

We avoid acronyms without context. Here is exactly how we explain the most common terms.

SOC (Security Operations Center)

Your digital control room. It monitors alerts and helps decide what is a real threat and what is noise.

MSSP (Managed Security Service Provider)

An external security team that supports your company daily with monitoring and incident response guidance.

Pentest

An authorized simulated attack that shows which weaknesses are truly exploitable and must be fixed first.

SIEM

A central screen that collects logs from many tools so suspicious behavior can be seen in one place.

EDR

A security sensor on each computer that detects unusual behavior and helps isolate compromised devices quickly.

Phishing and Ransomware

Phishing tricks users into giving access. Ransomware encrypts files to block operations until payment is demanded.
